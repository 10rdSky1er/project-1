President M. Russell Ballard

President M. Russell Ballard passed away today, we will explore his life both as an apostle and before.

Here's a Timeline of President Ballard's Life

  • 1928Born October 8, 1928, in Salt Lake Utah, USA to Melvin Russell Ballard and his wife, Geraldine Smith.
  • 1948Ballard served as a missionary in England from 1948 to 1950, during which he was a counselor in the mission presidency
  • 1950 From 1950 to mid-1960 he worked at and owned or resided in several businesses that ranged from automotive, real estate, and investment. He was the first Edesl car dealer and president of the Valley Music Hall although both ended up being financial losses.
  • 1951Married his wife of 67 years, Barbara Bowen, whom he had met while they were both studying at the University of Utah.
  • 1957Ballard retires from the US military reserve as a First Lieutenant.
  • 1974Ballard was called as president of the church's Canada Toronto Mission.
  • 1976While serving as a mission president, he was called to the First Quorum of the Seventy. He finished his three-year assignment as a mission president, while also fulfilling his duties as a member of the seventy.
  • 1980He served as the editor of the church's magazines from the start of 1980 until the end of 1984.
  • 1980He was a member of the Presidency of the Seventy from 1980 until 1985.
  • 1985Following the death of apostle Bruce R. McConkie, Ballard was sustained to the Quorum of the Twelve Apostles on October 6, 1985, and ordained an apostle on October 10, 1985.
  • 2018Following the January 2018 death of church president Thomas S. Monson, the church's First Presidency was reorganized with Russell M. Nelson as president. As the next senior apostle not in the First Presidency, Ballard became the quorum's acting president.
  • 2023Passed away in Salt Lake City on November 12, 2023, at the age of 95.
